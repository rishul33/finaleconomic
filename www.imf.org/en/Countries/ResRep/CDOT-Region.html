Alexandros Mourmouras,

Director

Alex joined CDOT in September 2020, after spending a year as fellow in the South East Asian Studies Program at the Center for Strategic and International Studies in Washington, DC. He has been on the IMF staff for 27 years, most recently as Division Chief and Mission Chief in the Asia Pacific Department during 2012-18. In this capacity, Alex led divisions that covered numerous ASEAN countries, was mission chief for Vietnam, Singapore and Malaysia, and coordinated closely with ASEAN and ASEAN plus three institutions. Prior to APD, Alex worked in the IMF’s Institute for Capacity Development during 2003-2012, designing and delivering training courses for officials. During 1999-2003, while on the staff of the Strategy, Policy and Review Department, Alex engaged in reviews of IMF policies, with a focus on the relationship between IMF conditionality and country ownership of reforms. Alex began his IMF career as economist in the Fiscal Affairs Department in the 1990s, participating in missions on surveillance, program work, and Technical Assistance.

Alex has published widely in macroeconomics, economic growth, international economics, and the political economy of international organizations. He served as consultant at the Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis and was fellow of the Institute for Advanced Study, University of Indiana. Prior to joining the IMF, he was Associate Professor and Director of Graduate Studies at the University of Cincinnati in the USA. He holds a PhD in Economics from the University of Minnesota and a BA degree in Applied Mathematics from Harvard College. He is a citizen of Greece.

Suhas Joshi,

Regional Treasury Advisor

Mr. Suhas Joshi joined CDOT in May 2019 as the Regional Treasury Advisor, covering Cambodia, Lao P.D.R., Myanmar, and Vietnam. Previously, he was a regional public financial management (PFM) advisor for the IMF in the Caribbean. Since joining the Fund in 1999, Suhas has worked in a diverse set of PFM areas in more than 40 countries, with a first assignment as a PFM advisor in Russia. He subsequently worked as a regional PFM advisor for West Africa, in the Pacific Financial and Technical Assistance Center (PFTAC) in Fiji, and for South and Southeast Asia based in Cambodia and covering 12 countries.

Prior to the IMF, Suhas worked for 18 years in senior government positions in India. He holds an MBA and an M. Sc. in National Development and Project Planning from the United Kingdom.

 

Nataliya Ivanyk,

Regional External Sector Statistics Advisor

Nataliya Ivanyk joined CDOT in November 2019 as the Regional External Sector Statistics (ESS) Advisor. Her project aims to build capacity to compile a full range of reliable ESS to ensuring sound monitoring of external risk and vulnerabilities and effective decision-making. Under the project, which commenced in August 2017, nine beneficiary countries in the Asia-Pacific region are eligible for support (Cambodia, Lao P.D.R., Myanmar, Vietnam, Nauru, Papua New Guinea, Timor–Leste, Samoa, and Tonga).

Ms. Ivanyk was previously a Senior Economist in the IMF Statistics Department’s Balance of Payments (BOP) Division. During her 20 years at the IMF, she has been engaged in developing ESS and national accounts statistics (NAS), for more than 40 countries through technical assistance and statistical quality assessment missions, two terms as a regional statistics advisor in the Middle East and Central Asia (ESS) and in Europe (NAS), and work on methodological issues and training material. Prior to joining the Fund, Nataliya headed the BOP Department and was Deputy Director of the Directorate of Economics at the National Bank of Ukraine. She holds a PhD in Economics from the Ukrainian Academy of Science and has published on various macroeconomic and statistical topics.                                                      

Sean Craig,

Macroeconomic Advisor

Richard Sean Craig (Sean) joined in September 2020 as the Macroeconomic Advisor on CDOT’s project on Integrating Macro-Financial Analysis into Macroeconomic Management. He brings a wealth of experience from 20 years at the IMF, 4 years at the Bank for International Settlements, and 10 years in the Federal Reserve Board’s International Division. He comes from the Asian and Pacific Department where he worked on the Regional Economic Outlook and has been a member of mission teams to Myanmar, Thailand, Malaysia, Korea and the Solomon Islands. He also served as the IMF’s Resident Representative in Hong Kong SAR.

Sean brings wide Fund experience to CDOT. He served in the Research and the Strategy, Policy and Review Departments, and in the Monetary and Capital Markets Department where he worked for 5 years on the Global Financial Stability Report, including several in the IMF’s London Office. Sean’s recent research has focused on monetary and macro-prudential policies, managing capital flow in Asia and policies to support corporate sector restructuring as Asia recovers from the pandemic. He received a Ph.D. and Masters of Political Economy from Boston University and a B.A. from the Claremont Colleges in California.

Delphine Anne Moretti,

Regional Public Financial Management (PFM) Advisor for Southeast Asia

Ms. Moretti has been based at CDOT since January 2021. In her capacity of Regional PFM Advisor, she is working with emerging economies (Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, and Thailand) and developing ones (Cambodia, Lao PDR, Myanmar, and Vietnam), focusing on fiscal management and forecasting, budget planning and execution, public investment management, and fiscal risk management, as well as other PFM topics.

Prior to this, Ms. Moretti served in the International Monetary Fund and OECD, where she assisted in advanced, emerging and developing countries in defining their PFM reforms roadmaps and introducing a range of budgetary reforms. She also developed and delivered large capacity development programmes.

Within the OECD, she led the OECD Senior Officials Network on Financial Management and Reporting and had a leadership role in developing research and coordinating seminars on advanced budget management practices, such as public sector balance sheet analysis, fiscal risks management and fiscal planning across government, as well as fiscal transparency, including modernisation of financial functions and digitalisation of financial reporting. 

Ms. Moretti worked previously for the French government. She also teached public financial management at the French School of Government (Ecole Nationale d’Administration, ENA).

Giorgi Barbakadze,

Monetary and Foreign Exchange Operations Advisor

Giorgi Barbakadze joined CDOT in January 2021 as the Regional Monetary and Foreign Exchange Operations Advisor, covering Cambodia, Lao P.D.R., Myanmar, and Vietnam. Previously, he was Resident Foreign Exchange Operations Advisor in Myanmar, and prior that he participated in several IMF TA missions as a short-term expert dealing with monetary policy frameworks, liquidity forecasting and management, and other monetary and FX issues.

Prior to the IMF, Giorgi worked for 20 years in several senior positions in monetary and fiscal authorities in Georgia, including member of Monetary Policy Committee and Head of the Monetary Policy Department at the Central Bank, Deputy Head of State Treasury and State Advisor to the Finance Minister. He has also lectured at several universities. Giorgi holds an MA in Policy Economics from Williams College in the U.S.A. and degrees in Economics and Law from Iv. Javakhishvili Tbilisi State University.

Deon Tanzer,

Regional Government Finance Statistics (GFS) and Public Sector Debt Statistics (PSDS) Advisor

Deon Tanzer joined CDOT in August 2022 as the Regional Government Finance Statistics (GFS) and Public Sector Debt Statistics (PSDS) Advisor. His project focuses on development and dissemination of high-quality fiscal data for effective macro-fiscal policy analyses. The project covers Cambodia, Lao PDR, Vietnam, Malaysia, Philippines, Indonesia and Thailand.

Deon has broad experience as a GFS and PSDS implementation advisor, starting his statistics career at Statistics Netherlands in 2006. Since 2013, Deon has been working with the IMF, initially at IMF headquarters, and the last 6 years in South-East Europe as a Regional Advisor. As implementation leader, researcher, and compiler, Deon has built extensive experience in GFS and the broader national accounts as well as first-hand experience in fiscal surveillance work. As GFS advisor, Deon has worked with officials across compiling institutions in various countries to comprehensively develop GFS per international standards and reporting requirements. Beyond IMF GFS work, Deon has collaborated with various international organizations and national governments to develop custom projects.

Wanicha Direkudomsak,

Economist

Wanicha Direkudomsak joined CDOT in January 2021 under the secondment of the Bank of Thailand (BOT), where she has worked for 7 years. She was previously a Senior Investment Officer in Reserve Management Department (RMD), proposing investment strategy to mitigate macroeconomic risks and to improve risk-adjusted return of international reserves. Wanicha provided hands-on assessment of economic fundamentals, monetary policy outlook and financial markets in the U.S. and Asian countries. Prior to RMD, she served as a Senior Economist in Monetary Policy Department. Having worked there for 3 years, she published on inflation dynamics topics in which her findings have been drawn upon in formulating policy recommendations for the BOT.

Wanicha also conducted research studies proposing institutional reforms to address rising inequality and corruption threats. She often gives lecture at universities, sharing how to apply theoretical knowledge in practice. Wanicha holds Master of Public Administration from Columbia University and M.A. in Economics from Thammasat University.

Economist

Isaravut Iamviriyakul joined CDOT in August 2020 under the secondment from the Bank of Thailand (BOT). He mainly supports CDOT’s delivery and monitoring of capacity development on macroeconomic management and its integration with surveillance work in the region.

Isaravut has worked for the BOT for seven years on monetary policy and international cooperation issues, with experience in international macroeconomic analysis in support of monetary policy decisions and a wide range of global issues related to the IMF and Bank for International Settlement. He gained regional exposure through representation in ASEAN Working Committees on Capital Account Liberalization and Capital Market Development and engagement with regional bodies like the Asian Development Bank and the South East Asia Central Banks (SEACEN) Centre. Isaravut also oversaw a number of regional financial initiatives, including the use of local currencies, promotion of sustainable finance, and enhancement of cybersecurity cooperation. He holds a Master’s Degree in Public Affairs from Sciences Po Paris.

At a Glance 

  • Established in Bangkok in September 2012, originally as the IMF Technical Assistance Office for Lao P.D.R. and Myanmar (TAOLAM).
  • Main beneficiaries: The core beneficiary countries are Myanmar, Lao P.D.R., Cambodia and Vietnam. Select capacity development projects based in CDOT also cover other countries in Southeast Asia and in the Pacific Island region.
  • External donors: Thailand and Japan. The Bank of Thailand hosts the CDOT Office. The Japan Administered Account for Selected IMF Activities (JSA) supports project activity in public financial management, monetary and foreign exchange operations, government finance statistics, external sector statistics, and macroeconomic management.
  • Capacity development is delivered to the beneficiary countries by a group of regional advisors, working closely with and supported by IMF functional departments, TA missions and training providers, and resident advisors and short-term experts. These advisors also collaborate with regional development partners.
  • Regional collaboration: CDOT also works in tandem with other IMF offices in the region involved in capacity development, including the IMF-Singapore Regional Training Institute , South Asia Training and Technical Assistance Center, and Regional Office for Asia and the Pacific in Tokyo.

Statement on the IMF’s Capacity Development Office in Thailand (CDOT)

September 11, 2018

Effective September 10, 2018, the IMF’s Technical Assistance Office for the Lao P.D.R. and the Republic of the Union of Myanmar (TAOLAM) located in Bangkok was renamed to the IMF Capacity Development Office in Thailand (CDOT). The name change reflects the complementary role that training plays with technical assistance, as the two pillars of capacity development at the IMF. As in recent years, CDOT will continue to focus on providing technical assistance and training to Lao P.D.R. and Myanmar, as well as Cambodia and Vietnam, as its core beneficiary countries. The principal objective of CDOT will remain to strengthen capacity in macroeconomic management and statistics in support of countries’ reform priorities. CDOT’s activities are made possible through direct financial support from the Government of Japan and an in-kind contribution from the Bank of Thailand.

Regional Economic Outlook

October 19, 2021

The Asian outlook for 2021 has been downgraded by more than 1 percent to 6.5 percent compared with the April 2021 World Economic Outlook because of new peaks of the pandemic cycle driven by the highly contagious Delta variant. As vaccination rates accelerate, the region is expected to grow slightly faster in 2022 than anticipated earlier. Although Asia and Pacific remains the fastest growing region in the world, the divergence between Asian advanced economies and emerging market and developing economies is deepening, reflecting vaccination coverage and policy support, and medium-term output levels in emerging market and developing economies are expected to remain below pre-pandemic trends. Risks are tilted to the downside, mainly because of uncertain pandemic dynamics, vaccine efficacy against virus variants, supply chain disruptions, and potential global financial spillovers from US monetary normalization in the presence of domestic financial vulnerabilities.
